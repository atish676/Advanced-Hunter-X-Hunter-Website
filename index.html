Top 5 Chimera Ants

Here we look at the 5 most powerful Chimera Ants
in the whole series

Top 5 Chimera Ants

As seen throughout the Chimera Ant arc, they're all incredibly powerful. Upon learning about Nen, they grow much stronger than they already were at birth. So far, we've seen quite a bunch of them and here's a list of the 5 strongest of them all.

  1. Shaiapouf

    Shaiapouf, commonly known as Pouf, was one of the Royal Guards of the King of Chimera Ants. His strength surpassed that of an average Hunter by a mile and even someone of Morel's caliber couldn't take him on.

  2. Menthuthuyoupi

    Another one of the King's Royal Guards, Menthuthuyoupi, simply known as Youpi, was just as strong as Pouf, if not stronger. His aura was tremendous and even multiple Hunters struggled to take him down, showcasing his superiority over others.

  3. Neferpitou

    Neferpitou was also one of Meruem's Royal Guards and their abilities lead us to believe that they had a slight edge over the other guards. Pitou's physicality was second to none and their powers were great enough to take down Kite, one of the strongest Hunters in the entire series.

  4. Chimera Ant Queen

    The Chimera Ant Queen was the first Chimera Ant to have been introduced to us. She was the mother of Meruem, and although she might not have been stronger than the Royal Guard of Meruem, she did have higher authority than them, which automatically makes her stronger than them in a sense.

  5. Meruem

    Meruem was the strongest known character to have ever appeared in the Hunter x Hunter series. He was also known as the King of the Chimera Ants and his skills were unmatched. Meruem's Nen abilities made him unstoppable, and even Netero, the strongest known Hunter in the series, wasn't able to defeat him.

5 Hisoka Facts you probably didn't knew

Hunter x Hunter is packed with strange characters, and Hisoka Morow's among the strangest. Here are five facts about him fans commonly miss out on.

  1. He's Rivaled Only By Killua
    (In Terms Of Clothing)

    Hisoka is a man of many talents and many ensembles. Observant fans have been aware of this for years, but casual viewers might not realize that the magician modifies his appearance at the beginning of each story arc. While his overall look remains consistent, the colors are altered and he'll sometimes add an accessory or two. The only character that challenges Hisoka in this category is Killua Zoldyck. The young assassin changes his look so often that he has become somewhat of a meme as a result.

  2. Pariston and Hisoka's relationship

    Hisoka and Pariston Hill have one thing in common, though it has nothing to do with the Hunter x Hunter storyline itself. Hiroki Takahashi, the clown's voice actor from the 1999 series, doubles as the voice behind Pariston Hill in the 2011 adaptation.

  3. Machi and Hisoka's relationship

    In the anime, Hisoka asked Phantom Troupe member Machi to have dinner with him after his fight with Kastro. As it turns out, the HxH manga painted a slightly different scenario that was ultimately rewritten for television. In print, Hisoka had actually asked Machi to spend the night with him, entirely skipping the invitation to wine and dine.

  4. Is Hisoka a member of Gon's gang?

    This is more of a fan theory than a confirmed fact. The four protagonists seen above all share a curious detail regarding their birthdays. Leorio was born on March 3 (3/3), Kurapika's birthday is on April 4 (4/4), Gon's falls on May 5 (5/5), and Killua's date of birth is July 7 (7/7). Hisoka was born on June 6 (6/6), thus completing the sequence. Because of this, astute fans have decided that the jester is the unofficial fifth protagonist of the series.

  5. Bungee Gum

    In the 1999 version of the anime, Hisoka states that he enjoyed "Bungee Gum" as a child, but since his family was poor, he would always chew it until the flavor was gone. He then named his signature move after the gum. This differs slightly from what we're told in both the manga and the 2011 adaptation where Hisoka simply named Bungee Gum and Texture Surprise after his favorite brands of gum and candy, respectively.

5 Hisoka Facts you probably didn't knew - Part 2

Here we look at 5 more Hisoka facts
fans didn't knew about

5 more Hisoka Facts you probably didn't knew

Here are five more facts about him fans commonly miss out on.

  1. Hisoka's intro at the Hunter's Exam

    The 2011 Hunter x Hunter anime features the wannabe Hunter's arms disintegrate into flower petals. He had bumped into Hisoka without apologizing, thus triggering the unfortunate altercation. This encounter didn't happen at all in the 1999 adaptation.The original outcome, depicted in the manga, was leaps and bounds more violent: the clown had retaliated by mercilessly severing the man's arms.

  2. Another clown

    Yoshihiro Togashi is the creator of both Yu Yu Hakusho and Hunter x Hunter. Hakusho's Suzuki would occasionally take the form of a clown with both a small teardrop and a heart painted on his face. Suzuki also profits from noteworthy card-throwing skills, which reminds us of our favorite morally ambiguous clown in HxH.

  3. Hisoka's Seiyuu

    Daisuke Namikawa, Hisoka's most prominent voice actor, is also the voice behind another Togashi creation: Prince Baka from Level E. However, the iconic actor has appeared in just about every other top-tier franchise or anime you could possibly think of. Naruto, Boruto, Bleach, One Piece, Demon Slayer, Jojo's Bizarre Adventure... well, the list goes on and on. He even voices the Sekiro from 2019's award-winning video game Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice.

  4. Hisoka's badge number 44

    Hisoka's Hunter Exam number badge bore the number 44. He also joined the Phantom Troupe after defeating Omokage, which automatically made the magician member #4 in the crew. In Japan, the number 4 has been deemed unlucky because the word for "four" is remarkably similar to the word for "death" in their language. Killing is one of Hisoka's specialties, so the number association is very fitting.

  5. Hisoka's Past

    Hisoka's Past is a Hunter x Hunter prequel that centers on the infamous magician and his life before the events of Togashi's series. Perhaps the most fascinating aspect is that this one-shot was written and illustrated by none other than Sui Ishida, Tokyo Ghoul's creator. You can read the same in our "Manga Section".

With this much range in abilities, and careful planning he does stand a chance against Meruem if he has an ability complete counter of Meruem's. He has a lot of aura because he used 7 different Nen abilities to battle Hisoka.

Zeno Zoldyck is Grandfather of Killua Zoldyck and so powerful that he claims to defeat Chrollo within 7 seconds. He is an Enhancer and his Nen ability 'Hyakushiki Kannon' was powerful enough to tackle Meruem head on. In the end but it was the bomb Miniature Rose who blasted Meruem to pieces but still survived and revived by his Royal Guards. If it were not them, he would've died of the poison in the air. So, Netero was actually capable to KILL Meruem.

Top 5 Hunter X Hunter Fights Ranked

Here we look at the best 5 fights Hunter X Hunter has

Top 5 Hunter X Hunter Fights Ranked

  1. Uvogin Vs. The Shadow Beasts

    During the Yorkness City Arc, the Shadow Beasts in this fight entered. The aggressive power of Rabid Dog, the sharpness of Porcupine, the parasitic nature of Leech, and incredible flexibility of worm, bringing in a diverse team of Nen experts that would give just about anyone else a run for their money. Still Uvo won.

  2. Kurapika Vs. Uvogin

    Kurapika's aim throughout the series has been to hunt down the Phantom Troupe members as revenge for their slaughter of his people, the Kurta Clan. Kurapika's fight shows his limitations and gauges the exact extent he is willing to sacrifice for his goal. The result of this is a brutal fight that showcases his new powers, as well as the intelligence and focus that he's taking into his plan.

  3. The Greed Island Dodgeball Match

    The Greed Island Arc served as the premise for a few video game gimmicks as well as an opportunity to organically build a training arc for Gon and Killua. The culmination of those efforts as well as everything the series has taught about Nen thus far is showcased in this dodgeball match between the unlikely alliance of Gon's friends and Hisoka going up against a killer dodgeball player.

  4. Netero Vs. Meruem

    One of the greatest scenes to arrive from the Chimera Ant Arc is the rollercoaster of strength and intelligence that is Netero vs. Meruem. It's such a tight match between two top tier characters that the result inevitably ends up to endurance, focus, and a nuclear bomb.

  5. Hisoka Vs. Chrollo Lucifer

    Unfortunately, the final and best entry on this list is one that has yet to make the anime. As a reward for giving him back his powers, Chrollo Lucifer has taken up Hisoka's request for a fight to the death. The very people who paid money to see this were subjected to mind control, explosions, and the involuntary subtraction of limbs, as two remorseless fighters used everything in their arsenal to get the kill.
